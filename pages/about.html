Modular Compliance was born from a simple frustration: NZ businesses were either paying enormous consultant fees to stay compliant, or guessing — and getting it wrong. We built the alternative.

The problem with compliance in NZ

New Zealand has a complex and frequently evolving compliance landscape. A small business might have obligations under the Health and Safety at Work Act, the Employment Relations Act, the Privacy Act 2020, and the Fair Trading Act — simultaneously. Each has its own requirements, its own risk of breach, and its own consequences for non-compliance.

The traditional answer was to hire consultants — expensive, episodic, and rarely proactive. You'd get a compliance review once a year, pay several thousand dollars, and hope nothing changed in the meantime. Of course, things always changed.

Generic overseas compliance platforms exist, but they're built around UK GDPR, US SOX, or global ISO standards. Retrofitting them to NZ law requires constant manual interpretation — work you end up paying someone to do anyway.

We built Modular Compliance as the platform we wished existed: purpose-built around NZ legislation, continuously monitored by AI, and priced for NZ SMEs — not multinational enterprises.

NZ Legislation

The law we're built on

Every module maps directly to a specific NZ Act — not a generic interpretation.

Health and Safety at Work Act 2015

PCBU duties, worker engagement, hazard management, incident notification, and WorkSafe obligations.

Privacy Act 2020

All 12 Information Privacy Principles, privacy breach notification, and Privacy Commissioner obligations.

AML/CFT Act 2009

Customer due diligence, enhanced due diligence, suspicious transaction reporting, and DIA/FMA/RBNZ obligations.

Employment Relations Act 2000

Employment agreements, good faith obligations, personal grievance processes, and holiday pay calculations.

Resource Management Act 1991

Resource consent conditions, emissions limits, environmental incident reporting, and council notification requirements.

Building Act 2004 & Unit Titles Act 2010

Building consents, code compliance, LBP requirements, and body corporate governance obligations.
